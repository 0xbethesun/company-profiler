## 1. The Org

### What it is
Stripe is the financial infrastructure company built by Patrick and John Collison. **5 million+ businesses run on Stripe** including 90% of the Dow Jones Industrial Average, 80% of the Nasdaq 100, half of the Fortune 100, and all of the top AI companies. [Stripe 2025 annual letter](https://stripe.com/newsroom/news/stripe-2025-update) [Sacra](https://sacra.com/c/stripe/)

Self-stated positioning. *"Financial infrastructure to grow your revenue. Accept payments, offer financial services, and implement custom revenue models, from your first transaction to your billionth."* [stripe.com](https://stripe.com/)

### Founding and key dates
- Founded 2010 by Irish brothers Patrick Collison (CEO) and John Collison (President), in Palo Alto. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stripe,_Inc.)
- Seed funding of $2M in 2011 from Elon Musk, Peter Thiel, and others. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stripe,_Inc.)
- Total funding since founding. ~$9.4B. [Sacra](https://sacra.com/c/stripe/)
- October 2025. Second Dublin headquarters opened. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stripe,_Inc.)
- February 2026. **$159B valuation via tender offer**, up from $91.5B in Feb 2025 (74% increase). Investors providing the bulk include Thrive Capital, Coatue, a16z. Stripe also using its own capital to repurchase shares. [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/24/stripes-valuation-soars-74-to-159-billion/) [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/24/stripe-value-stock-sale-tender-offer.html) [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stripe,_Inc.)
- Largest privately-owned fintech company globally. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stripe,_Inc.)

### The 2026 strategic narrative. "Economic infrastructure for AI"
288 launches at Stripe Sessions 2026, framed as building the economic infrastructure for AI. [Sessions 2026 press](https://stripe.com/newsroom/news/sessions-2026)

- **Agentic Commerce Suite.** Single integration that lets businesses sell their products inside AI apps. Kate Spade, Best Buy, and Coach already building with it. [Sessions 2026 press](https://stripe.com/newsroom/news/sessions-2026)
- **Agent-ready financial accounts.** Agents can check balances, pay invoices, store funds, create cards, send money, and manage cash flow with human-in-the-loop confirmation for key actions. [Sessions 2026 press](https://stripe.com/newsroom/news/sessions-2026)
- **Link.** Consumer wallet with **250M+ users globally**, now ready for AI agents. [Sessions 2026 press](https://stripe.com/newsroom/news/sessions-2026)
- **Streaming payments.** AI-native business model combining Metronome usage tracking with stablecoin micropayments on the Tempo blockchain. [Sessions 2026 press](https://stripe.com/newsroom/news/sessions-2026)
- **Digital asset accounts** (with Privy). Infrastructure for stablecoin-based financial products. [Sessions 2026 press](https://stripe.com/newsroom/news/sessions-2026)
- **Stablecoin-backed cards via Stripe Issuing.** Live in 30 countries, targeting 100+ by end of 2026. [Sessions 2026 press](https://stripe.com/newsroom/news/sessions-2026)
- **Microsoft Copilot integration.** New shopping experiences. [Sessions 2026 press](https://stripe.com/newsroom/news/sessions-2026)

## 5. Strategic Angles (raw)

### A. The thesis in one line
Stripe is making the bet that **the economy itself runs on programmable money** and that **agents are the next major class of economic actor**. Every 2026 launch maps to one of those two bets. [Sessions 2026 press](https://stripe.com/newsroom/news/sessions-2026)

### B. The Collisons' read on agent commerce is calibrated, not maximalist
The annual letter explicitly says agentic commerce is *"overhyped too early in some corners"* and the industry is *"hovering on the edge of levels 1 and 2"*. Patrick on Dwarkesh framed the transition as *"an almost unnoticeably smooth continuum"*. This is calibrated builder behavior, not hype-cycle behavior. **Anyone pitching Stripe leadership on agents wins by being calibrated, not maximalist.**

### C. The moat is operational, not technological
Patrick is direct. The moat is *"people at Stripe really care about solving the problems."* 1,000 production deploys per day at 99.9995% reliability is a process feat, not an algorithm feat. **The operating system of the company is the product.** Operators who want to work with Stripe are buying into that operating-excellence aesthetic, not a feature set.

### D. The international gravity is real
57% of new 2025 companies are non-US. The Lloyds, French, AWS, and Deel partnerships in the last 90 days are all GTM-multiplier moves outside the US core. *"The 'long tail' is much of the dog."* International is where the next billion in TPV comes from.

### E. Stablecoins are no longer a side bet
30-to-100 country expansion in Issuing. Streaming payments built on Tempo. Bridge ($1.1B), Privy, and Metronome stitched into a stack. This is a 12-month operational sprint, not an R&D experiment.

### F. Where an operator would contribute (general hypotheses)
- **Partner program operations.** The Sessions 2026 launch list implies a partner-facing operations function under enormous load. Onboarding, certification, GTM enablement.
- **Internal AI tooling adoption.** Patrick's "millions of invocations per day" implies an enablement function getting employees onto the tooling. People Ops and L&D at this scale would touch this.
- **International compliance ops.** The 100-country stablecoin push runs through licensing, KYC, and regulatory operations. High-leverage operator role.

### G. Where Stripe is automating
Public clues from Patrick's Dwarkesh interview. Stripe runs an internal LLM platform doing millions of daily invocations across financial-services use cases (analog-to-digital conversion, customer support, sales tooling). The Collisons consistently frame AI as augmentation, not replacement. *"What would we elect to do much more of?"* — Patrick at Retool Summit 2025.
