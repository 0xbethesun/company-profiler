# Stripe Brain Map

*Strategic read for prep ahead of an outreach or partnership conversation with Stripe leadership. Built 2026-06-24.*

## TLDR
- Stripe is the financial infrastructure company built by **Patrick (CEO) and John Collison (President)**. Founded 2010, dual HQ in South San Francisco and Dublin, ~8,500 employees, ~$9.4B raised total.
- **$159B valuation** (Feb 2026 tender offer, up 74% from $91.5B a year prior). **$6.93B revenue in 2025 (+36% YoY)**, **$1.2B EBITDA, robustly profitable. $1.9T total payment volume (+34% YoY)**, 5M+ businesses, 90% of the Dow Jones, 80% of the Nasdaq 100, half of the Fortune 100, and all top AI companies.
- The 2026 strategic narrative is **"economic infrastructure for AI."** 288 launches at Sessions 2026, all mapping to two bets. agents as a new class of economic actor, and stablecoins as the rails underneath.
- The Collisons are **calibrated, not maximalist** on agent commerce. Their annual letter explicitly says agentic commerce is *"overhyped too early in some corners"* and the industry is *"hovering on the edge of levels 1 and 2."* Pitching them on agents wins by being calibrated.
- The moat is operational, not technological. Patrick's words. *"In as much as Stripe has a moat, people at Stripe really care about solving the problems."* **1,000 production deploys per day at 99.9995% reliability** is a process feat. The operating system of the company is the product.
- The single best opening line for an outreach conversation. **"I want to understand how a builder I respect thinks about the boring infrastructure underneath the agent-commerce story, because that is where I would actually want to contribute."**
- Highest-signal section to study before the meeting. Section 4 (Strategic Angles).

## 1. The Org

### What it is
Stripe is the financial infrastructure layer for 5 million businesses, including most of the largest companies in the world. From the Collisons' 2025 annual letter.

*"Our programmable financial services now power more than 5 million businesses directly or via platforms, including all of the top AI companies, many of the largest blue-chip companies (90% of the Dow Jones Industrial Average), most of the biggest tech companies (80% of the Nasdaq 100)."*

Self-stated positioning. *"Financial infrastructure to grow your revenue. Accept payments, offer financial services, and implement custom revenue models, from your first transaction to your billionth."*

### Founding and key dates
- Founded 2010 by Irish brothers Patrick and John Collison in Palo Alto.
- Initial $2M seed in 2011 from Elon Musk and Peter Thiel among others.
- October 2025. Second Dublin headquarters opened.
- **February 2026. $159B valuation via tender offer**, up from $91.5B in Feb 2025 (74%). Investors providing the liquidity include Thrive Capital, Coatue, a16z. Stripe also using its own capital to buy back shares.
- Largest privately-owned fintech globally. The Collisons have publicly resisted going public for years, and the tender structure is read as a stay-private signal.

### Core products (24+ surfaces)
Payments (Checkout, Payment Links, Elements, Link, Managed Payments), Connect, Billing (Subscriptions, Invoicing, Revenue Recognition, Usage-based Billing), Capital, Issuing, Treasury, Atlas, Radar, Tax, Identity, Terminal, Sigma, Data Pipeline, Financial Connections, Global Payouts, Crypto and Crypto Onramp.

### Business performance (2025 actuals)
- **$1.9T total payment volume.** Up 34% YoY.
- **$6.93B revenue.** Up 36% YoY.
- **$1.2B EBITDA.** Robustly profitable.
- Revenue and Finance Automation Suite on track for **$1B annual run rate in 2026.**
- 200M active subscriptions managed via Billing.
- 350+ product updates shipped in 2025.
- 57% of new customer companies based outside the US. 2025 cohort growing ~50% faster than 2024 cohort.
- Black Friday to Cyber Monday 2025 peak. 578M transactions, $40B+ volume.

### The 2026 strategic narrative. Economic infrastructure for AI
288 launches at Sessions 2026, framed as building the economic infrastructure for AI. Concrete pieces.

- **Agentic Commerce Suite.** Single integration that lets businesses sell their products inside AI apps. Kate Spade, Best Buy, and Coach already building with it.
- **Agent-ready financial accounts.** Agents can check balances, pay invoices, store funds, create cards, send money, and manage cash flow with human-in-the-loop confirmation for key actions.
- **Link.** Consumer wallet with **250M+ users globally**, now agent-ready.
- **Streaming payments.** AI-native business model combining Metronome usage tracking with stablecoin micropayments on the Tempo blockchain.
- **Digital asset accounts** (with Privy). Infrastructure for stablecoin-based financial products.
- **Stablecoin-backed cards via Stripe Issuing.** Live in 30 countries, targeting 100+ by end of 2026.
- **Microsoft Copilot integration.** New shopping experiences inside Copilot.

### Recent partnerships and milestones (last 90 days)
- **June 22, 2026.** AWS partnership for AI agent payments. Eileen O'Mara promoted to Vice Chair, Tyler Bryson named CRO.
- **June 16, 2026.** French business tour focused on AI economy.
- **June 10, 2026.** UK push to *"build for the AI economy."*
- **June 9, 2026.** Stripe powers Lloyds payment tools for UK small business.
- **June 3, 2026.** Deel chooses Stripe for stablecoin contractor wallets.

### Acquisitions powering the stablecoin and AI stack
- **Bridge** (stablecoin platform). $1.1B, February 2025. The largest stablecoin acquisition to date.
- **Metronome** (usage-based billing). The Metronome stack now powers streaming payments.
- **Privy** (crypto wallets). Powers digital asset accounts.
- Earlier. Paystack (Nigeria, 2020), TaxJar (2021).

### Competitive position
Primary direct competitors. PayPal, Adyen, Checkout.com. Surface-specific challengers include Lithic (card issuing), Orb (usage billing), Ramp (corporate cards). The structural advantage. vertical integration across payments, billing, money movement, stablecoin rails, and now agent infrastructure. No competitor matches the surface area.

### Leadership
- **Patrick Collison, Co-founder and CEO.**
- **John Collison, Co-founder and President.**
- **Eileen O'Mara, Vice Chair.** Promoted from CRO June 22, 2026.
- **Tyler Bryson, Chief Revenue Officer.** Newly appointed June 22, 2026.

#### Voice. Direct quotes

On craft as a hiring filter.
- *"The best people consider themselves crafts people in their domain and they really, above almost all else, want to work with the best other people."*

On Silicon Valley's blind spot.
- *"Silicon Valley... doesn't place a lot of value on process and operational excellence."*

On Stripe's operating philosophy.
- *"The very earnest, repeated, serious, and long term application of taking the goal seriously."*

On AI internally.
- *"LLMs can be a surprisingly interesting augmenting tool."*
- Stripe runs an internal LLM platform doing *"millions of invocations per day"* across dozens of production use cases.

On the agentic transition.
- Change will follow *"some gradient... an almost unnoticeably smooth continuum up to very considerable degrees of autonomy."*

On the moment.
- *"Q1 2026 could be looked at as the first quarter of the singularity."*

#### Signal
Patrick treats Stripe as a craft business. Reliability gets explicit weight (the *"99.9995% reliability"* figure is a public point of pride). He invokes Conquest's third law (*"organizations behave as if run by a cabal of their enemies"*) as the frame for why mission alignment must be defended generationally. He thinks in decades. His quote on API design references the *"NS"* prefix in Cocoa as an example of multi-decadal abstractions that compound over time. **Stripe wins by building long.**

## 3. Strategic Angles

### A. The thesis in one line
Stripe's 2026 bet is that **the economy itself runs on programmable money** and that **agents are the next major class of economic actor**. Every Sessions 2026 launch maps to one of those two bets. Anyone meeting with Stripe leadership should understand which side of that thesis matters most for the conversation at hand.

### B. The Collisons' read on agent commerce is calibrated, not maximalist
This is the single most important thing to internalize before a meeting. The annual letter explicitly calls agentic commerce *"overhyped too early in some corners"* and says the industry is *"hovering on the edge of levels 1 and 2."* Patrick frames the transition as *"an almost unnoticeably smooth continuum."* Stripe is shipping aggressively (Agentic Commerce Suite, Link, agent-ready accounts) while saying the loud parts quietly. **Match the register. Calibrated builders win in this room. Hype-cycle pitches lose.**

### C. The moat is operational, not technological
Patrick's framing is consistent and clear. Stripe's moat is people who care about the problem, supported by operational discipline that Silicon Valley underrates. **1,000 production deploys per day at 99.9995% reliability is a process feat, not an algorithm feat.** Whoever sits across from Stripe should treat this as a culture-of-craft business and pitch accordingly. Not vendor pitches. Not founder fanboy energy. Specific, sourced, operator-grade thinking.

### D. The international gravity is real
**57% of new 2025 companies are non-US.** The Lloyds, French, AWS, and Deel partnerships in the last 90 days are all international or AI-economy GTM-multiplier moves. The Collisons quote on the *"long tail"* being *"much of the dog"* is the strategic posture. International and non-top-10 economies are where the next billion in TPV comes from. Any cross-border conversation with Stripe should lead with that.

### E. Stablecoins are no longer a side bet
- Bridge ($1.1B), Privy, and Metronome stitched into a stack.
- Streaming payments built on Tempo.
- Issuing stablecoin cards. 30 countries, targeting 100+ by year end.
- Deel deploying stablecoin contractor wallets at scale.

This is a 12-month operational sprint, not an R&D experiment. Stripe is the most strategically committed stablecoin player among the legacy fintech incumbents.

### F. Where an operator would contribute (general hypotheses)
- **Partner program operations.** 288 launches at Sessions implies a partner-facing operations function under enormous load. Onboarding, certification, GTM enablement.
- **Internal AI tooling adoption.** Patrick's *"millions of invocations per day"* implies an enablement function getting employees onto internal LLM tooling.
- **International compliance operations.** The 100-country stablecoin push runs through licensing, KYC, and regulatory operations. A high-leverage operator function.
- **Acquisition integration.** Bridge, Metronome, and Privy are all recent and all material. People and process integration of those teams is live operational work.

### G. What Stripe is automating internally
Public clues from Patrick's Dwarkesh interview. Stripe runs an internal LLM platform doing millions of daily invocations across financial-services use cases. The Collisons consistently frame AI as augmentation, not replacement. Patrick at Retool Summit. *"What would we elect to do much more of?"*

## 4. High-Signal Deep Dive

### Why the Dwarkesh interview is the highest-leverage read
It is the single source that explains how Patrick thinks about craft, reliability, mission, and AI's effect on a payments business. The interview is the canonical brief on Stripe's worldview. Two hours, no slides, full register.

### Key reveals
- **The moat is people who care.** Said plainly, on the record. Anyone selling Stripe a story has to clear that bar.
- **Reliability is treated as a moral commitment.** 1,000 deploys per day at 99.9995%. Process and operational excellence are *"undervalued"* in Silicon Valley.
- **Stripe builds for decades.** The NS-prefix reference (Objective-C inheritance from 30 years ago) is a deliberate Stripe-builds-multi-decadal-abstractions signal.
- **AI is augmentation, not replacement.** Internal LLM tooling at millions of invocations per day, framed as letting people do more of what they want to do.

## 5. Smart Questions to Ask in a Meeting

### For Patrick or any senior Stripe operator
- *"Your annual letter framed agent commerce as overhyped in some corners. Where do you think the consensus story has it most wrong right now?"*
- *"You have said reliability is undervalued in Silicon Valley. What does that translate to operationally inside Stripe in 2026?"*
- *"Of the 288 launches at Sessions, which one do you expect to look most important in five years, and which one is most likely to look like a wrong turn?"*

### For John or revenue-facing leaders
- *"Where does the 'long tail is much of the dog' statement translate into operational priorities? Which non-top-10 economies are highest on the operating list?"*
- *"How is the Bridge integration affecting how Stripe builds product roadmaps across the rest of the stack?"*

### For the AI and infrastructure side
- *"What do you think other large companies are missing about internal AI deployment that Stripe figured out?"*
- *"Where does the human-in-the-loop confirmation cutoff actually sit for agent-ready financial accounts today?"*

---

## 6. What NOT to Do in a Meeting With Stripe
- Do not pitch agents in hype-cycle terms. The Collisons have publicly drawn the calibrated line. Match it.
- Do not gloss reliability or operational rigor. Patrick has made it the moat-defining feature on the record. Vague answers about *"scalable processes"* land as unserious.
- Do not assume Stripe is American-first. 57% of new 2025 companies are non-US, and the public posture is international-by-default.
- Do not treat stablecoins as a side experiment. The acquisitions and the Issuing rollout say otherwise.
- Do not lead with founder fanboy energy. The Collisons read widely and screen for substance fast. Bring an operator-grade point of view.
